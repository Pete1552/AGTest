How to play

Classic Stratego against the computer. You each command 40 pieces: ranks 1 (Spy) through 10 (Marshal), six Bombs, and the Flag — all hidden from your opponent until they fight. Tap a piece type below, then tap an empty cell in your own four rows to place it (tap a placed piece to pick it back up); Randomize all or Auto-place rest saves you the trouble. Once every piece is down, tap Start Battle. On your turn, tap one of your pieces to see where it can go, then tap a highlighted cell to move or attack — most pieces step one square, but the Scout (2) can dash any distance in a straight line. Whichever rank is higher wins a fight, and both pieces are revealed either way, win or lose. Two exceptions: the Spy can defeat the Marshal, but only if the Spy is the one attacking — the Marshal still wins if it attacks the Spy. And only a Miner (3) can safely clear a Bomb; anyone else who attacks one is destroyed instead. Capture the enemy Flag, or leave them with no piece able to move at all, and you win. Your score is how many turns the win took — fewer is better.
