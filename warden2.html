How to play

You are the keep's mounted warden, but the keep now runs its own economy. Tap a build icon (Farm, Storehouse, Barracks, Watchtower) then drag the ghost into place and confirm — building costs come out of your Gold. Gold isn't just looted from raiders any more: train a Miner at a Storehouse and it will haul ore from the gold veins scattered around the field back home, turning it into spendable gold on every trip — veins run thin the more you draw from one, so it pays to work more than one. Farms produce Provisions, the upkeep every building and soldier draws on; if you run short, training slows down, so keep a Farm or two ahead of your garrison. Tap a placed Barracks to train Footmen, Archers (range, never close in), Spearmen (harder hits, slower afoot) or Catapults (splash damage) — tap a Storehouse to train more Miners. Tap the stance button to switch your own weapon: Bow shoots at range on its own, while the Lance cuts down anyone you ride through. The enemy has a real Warcamp at the field's edge that musters raiding parties on its own over time instead of scripted waves — ride out and hold your ground beside it to raid it for bonus gold and set its production back, but the keep goes undefended while you're away. Tap a footman to select it, or drag a box over several to command them as a group; tap empty ground to deselect. Survive as long as you can — your score is how long you last.
