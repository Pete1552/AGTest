How to play

You command the keep from above now — there's no rider on the field, just your garrison and your economy. The keep sits in a much wider land, so drag with two fingers to look around, and use the minimap and the ⌖ button (which snaps the view back to the keep) to stay oriented. Unscouted ground is hidden; once a unit of yours has seen it it stays dimly remembered, but raiders vanish from sight again the moment nothing of yours can currently see them, so scouting matters. Tap a build icon (Farm, Storehouse, Barracks, Watchtower, Wall, Gate) then drag the ghost into place and confirm — it snaps to a grid, glowing red if that plot's already taken. Most buildings cost Gold; Walls and Gates cost Stone instead. Train a Miner at a Storehouse and it will haul from whichever gold vein or stone quarry has the most left, banking Gold or Stone on every trip — a node runs thin the more you draw from it, so it pays to work more than one, and a scout's report always marks the nearest vein for you at the start. Farms produce Provisions, the upkeep every building and soldier draws on; if you run short, training slows down, so keep a Farm or two ahead of your garrison. Tap a placed Barracks to train Footmen, Archers (range, never close in), Spearmen (harder hits, slower afoot) or Catapults (splash damage) — tap a Storehouse to train more Miners. You start with one Footman garrisoned at the keep. Tap a footman to select it, or drag a box over several to command them as a group, then tap the field to post them somewhere new — they'll fight anything that wanders into range on their own. Raiders can't cross a Wall, and a shut Gate blocks them just the same — tap a Gate to swing it open or closed. Wall off the keep with a gate or two left open and raiders are forced to funnel through wherever you left the way in, so stack your archers and towers there; leave every gate shut and they'll just force their way through the nearest wall instead of stalling forever. The enemy has a real Warcamp far off that musters raiding parties on its own over time instead of scripted waves — post some of your troops beside it and hold the ground to raid it for bonus gold and set its production back, but they aren't guarding the keep while they're there. Survive as long as you can — your score is how long you last.
