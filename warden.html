How to play

You are the keep's mounted warden. Drag to ride; charging raiders that reach the gate steal a heart, so meet them in the field. Tap the stance button to switch weapon: Bow shoots at range on its own, while the Lance cuts down anyone you ride through (no arrows — you must dive in). The enemy adapts to whatever you favour: lean on the bow and they send skirmishers, who dodge most arrows but fall to the lance; lean on the lance and they send pikemen, who brace against a charge but are soft to arrows. Shieldbearers block both weapons from the front — flank them. Brutes just soak hits. Every repelled raider drops gold. Between bands, spend it in the Muster: raise instant Footmen, invest in a Granary for more gold each band, or sharpen the warden — or drag a Watchtower or Barracks into place yourself before confirming. Tap a placed Barracks any time during a band to train a proper roster: Archers shoot from range without closing in, Spearmen hit harder but move slower, and Catapults lob a blast that hits everyone nearby — each takes a few seconds to train, so queue ahead. Only your selection answers your touch: tap a footman to select it, or drag a box over several to select them as a group, then tap the field to post them somewhere new — but while any are selected the horse holds her ground. Tap empty ground to deselect, or tap the horse to take the reins back before you can ride again. Survive as long as you can.
